The Golden Age of Indie

Game Development Talk at Droidcon UK, 2011

Sometime in June 2011 Thibaut Rouffineau invited me to give a talk at an Android-focused conference in London that he was co-organizing: Droidcon UK. I liked the size and developer-centric approach the team was aiming for, so after a little back and forth I signed up. It did help of course that Thibaut gave me free reign over picking a specific topic and style, as long as it was related to gaming. Seldomly was any requirement easier to meet :)

Fingerpaint, FTW

When putting together material for a talk on cloud computing I gave in late 2010, I used Autodesk's excellent Sketchbook Pro iPad app to draw a couple of slides. I was short on time and eager to also try another approach to presentation design, so I left it at those two initial ones, promising myself to come back to digital fingerpaint.
Creating the slide deck for my March/April 2011 talks on utilizing operational transforms in multiplayer-gaming was the first time that I resorted to hand-drawing pretty much everything. In October 2011 I followed up with the slides for The Golden Age of Indie.
